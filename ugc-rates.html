What is UGC and Why Does Pricing Work Differently?

UGC (user-generated content) means a brand pays you to create content they use in their own marketing — rather than posting it to your own audience. The brand runs it as a paid ad, posts it on their social channels, or uses it on their website.

This is a fundamentally different transaction to a sponsored post. You're not being paid for your audience reach — you're being paid for your production skills. Which means follower count is largely irrelevant. A UGC creator with 500 followers can charge the same as one with 500,000 if the content quality is there.

It also means usage rights are not an add-on — they're the entire point of the deal. The brand is buying your content to use it. This needs to be reflected in your rate.

🎯 The most underpriced category in the creator economy UGC creators consistently undercharge because there's no widely known benchmark. Unlike sponsored posts where creators can compare notes, UGC pricing is opaque — which is exactly why brands can get away with offering so little.

The rates above are base creation fees. Usage rights are separate and must be added on top. This is where most UGC creators massively undercharge — or give rights away entirely without realising.

UGC vs Sponsored Posts — Which Pays More?

For small creators (under 50k followers), UGC often pays more than sponsored posts — because your rate isn't capped by your follower count. You're selling production skills, not audience reach.

For larger creators (100k+), sponsored posts typically pay more because your audience reach adds significant value. But UGC is a strong supplementary income stream regardless of size — and it's entirely separate from your channel, so it doesn't affect your posting schedule or audience relationship.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I charge for UGC in 2026?
A beginner UGC creator should charge $150–$350 per short video (15–60s) plus usage rights. Experienced creators with a strong portfolio charge $350–$900 per video. Usage rights for paid ads add $150–$900 on top depending on duration.
Do UGC rates depend on follower count?
No — UGC rates are based on content quality and usage rights, not audience size. A creator with 1,000 followers can charge the same as one with 100,000 if their content quality is comparable. This makes UGC one of the most accessible income streams for smaller creators.
What are usage rights and why do they matter for UGC?
Usage rights determine how and for how long a brand can use your content. For UGC, usage rights are the entire commercial value — the brand is buying your content to run as paid ads or use on their channels. Always agree usage terms before starting work and charge separately for them.
Should I post UGC content on my own channel?
Not automatically — and not for free. Posting the content on your own channel as well is a separate deliverable (essentially a sponsored post) and should be priced separately. Many brands will ask for this as a bonus. It isn't one — it's an additional service.
