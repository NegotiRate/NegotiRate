How to Negotiate a TikTok Brand Deal

Most TikTok creators leave money on the table because they don't know how to push back without risking the deal. Here's what actually works:

1. Know your number before you respond. Don't reply to an offer without knowing your real rate first. Use our calculator to get a baseline before you engage.

2. Counter with data, not feelings. "Based on my average views and engagement, the market rate for this type of content is $X" lands far better than "I was hoping for more."

3. Ask about usage before you quote. Find out if they want to run it as an ad before you price. If they do, your rate needs to reflect that — quoting without knowing is how creators undercharge.

4. Don't apologise for your rate. Brands expect negotiation. Quoting confidently with a clear rationale is respected, not resented.

How much should a US TikTok creator charge for a brand deal?
US TikTok creators with 10k–50k followers typically charge $350–$900 per video. Creators with 50k–200k followers charge $900–$3,500. Above 200k, rates start at $3,500 and scale with average views and niche. Use the free NegotiRate calculator to get a personalised figure based on your exact stats.
Does TikTok follower count or views matter more for brand deals?
Views matter more. A creator with 20k followers averaging 100k views per video is worth significantly more than a creator with 200k followers averaging 5k views. Always lead with your average views when negotiating.
What is a good TikTok engagement rate for brand deals?
A good TikTok engagement rate is 4–8%. Above 8% is excellent and justifies a rate premium. Below 3% is considered weak. Engagement rate is calculated as (likes + comments + shares) ÷ views × 100.
Should I charge extra for TikTok usage rights?
Yes — always. If a brand wants to use your content in paid ads (whitelisting), charge 25–50% on top of your base rate per 30 days of usage. Many creators hand over rights for free without realising.
